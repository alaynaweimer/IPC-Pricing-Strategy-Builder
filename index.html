üéØ Why Pricing Strategy Matters

Underpricing is the #1 business killer. Most small business owners charge too little because they're afraid of losing clients. But here's the truth: you can't build a sustainable business on rates that don't cover your real costs.

Your price isn't just a number ‚Äî it signals your value, attracts the right clients, and determines whether you build wealth or just stay busy.

üí∞ Reality check: A 20% price increase on a $50/hr rate adds $10,400/year to your income (at 20 billable hours/week). Most clients won't even blink.

üéØ The Sweet Spot: Retainers

Most fractional and consulting businesses thrive on retainers. They provide predictable monthly income, deeper client relationships, and less time spent on proposals and invoicing. Aim for 2-4 retainer clients as your foundation.

üí° How to communicate it:

"Effective [date], my rates will be [new rate]. I'm grateful for our work together and committed to continuing to deliver excellent results." Keep it simple. Don't over-explain or apologize.

üíº Pro Tips From the Field

  • Quote high, negotiate if needed. You can always come down, but you can't go up. Start 20% higher than you think.
  • Never quote on the spot. "Let me put together a proposal" gives you time to price thoughtfully, not emotionally.
  • Price the client, not just the work. A Fortune 500 company can pay more than a startup for the same deliverable.
  • Include a "pain premium." Rush jobs, difficult clients, or boring work? Add 25-50% to your rate.
  • Offer 3 options. Budget, standard, premium. Most people pick the middle. Make the middle your ideal price.
  • Retainers should include a discount. 10-15% off hourly equivalent in exchange for predictable, guaranteed income.
